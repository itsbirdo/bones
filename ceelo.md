# Cee-lo

A fast gambling dice game played with **three six-sided dice**. Also spelled *Cee-lo*, *C-Lo*, *See-Low*, or *4-5-6*. It's a banker-vs-players game: quick rounds, lots of betting, money moves fast.

## Equipment

- **3 six-sided dice**
- A bowl, wall, or flat surface to roll against
- Money or chips to bet

A roll where any die lands cocked or leaning is a **no roll** — rethrow all three.

## Roles

- One player is the **banker** (the shooter who covers all bets).
- Everyone else bets against the bank.
- Players put up a wager; the banker must cover (match) it from the bank.

## Possible Roll Outcomes

You roll all three dice at once. The result is judged as follows.

### Instant wins
- **4-5-6** — automatic win. The highest possible roll.
- **Triples** (1-1-1, 2-2-2, … 6-6-6) — three of a kind. Automatic win, and triples **beat** a 4-5-6. Higher triples beat lower triples.

### Instant loss
- **1-2-3** — automatic loss. The worst roll.

### A "point" (pair + one odd die)
- If two dice match and the third differs, the **odd die is your point/score**.
  - e.g. 3-3-5 → point is **5**
  - e.g. 6-6-2 → point is **2**
- A point can be 1–6. **6 is the best** non-instant result; **1 is the weakest**.

### No decision (re-roll)
- Any roll that isn't one of the above (e.g. 2-3-4, 1-4-5) is a **nothing roll**. Roll again until you get a result.

## Roll Ranking (best → worst)

| Roll | Result |
|------|--------|
| Triples (6-6-6 highest → 1-1-1 lowest) | Automatic win (beats 4-5-6) |
| 4-5-6 | Automatic win |
| Point of 6 | Score 6 |
| Point of 5 → 2 | Score, descending |
| Point of 1 | Weakest score |
| 1-2-3 | Automatic loss |

## Round / Order of Play

1. **Banker rolls first.**
   - 4-5-6 or triple → banker wins all bets immediately.
   - 1-2-3 → banker loses, pays out all bets.
   - A point → that's the **bank's point**; players now try to beat it.
   - Nothing → re-roll.

2. **Each player rolls** (in turn), trying to beat the banker:
   - 4-5-6 or triple → player wins, banker pays.
   - 1-2-3 → player loses, banker takes the bet.
   - **Higher point** than the bank → player wins.
   - **Lower point** → player loses.
   - **Same point** → **push/tie** (no money changes hands).
   - Nothing → re-roll until a result.

3. **Settle bets.** Banker pays winners and collects from losers, one player at a time.

## Banker Rotation

Being banker is an advantage (you act first, and in many rules win ties), so the bank passes around. Common conventions:
- Bank passes to the next player when the **banker rolls 1-2-3** (instant loss), **or**
- After a set number of rounds, **or**
- When the banker chooses to pass it.

## Playing With 2 Players

Cee-lo works great head-to-head: one banker, one player, taking turns.

1. **Decide who banks first** (high roll, or just agree).
2. **Set the wager** per round.
3. **Banker rolls**, then **player rolls** and compares.
4. **Settle**, then next round.

To keep the banker edge fair, pick one:
- **Alternate the bank every round** — simplest and most balanced (recommended).
- **Pass the bank on a banker loss** — more traditional, streakier.
- **First-to-X / best-of** — fixed rounds each as banker, tally winnings.

## House Rules to Agree Up Front

- **Triples vs. 4-5-6:** Most rules say any triple beats a 4-5-6. Some casual games treat both as equal automatic wins.
- **Ties:** Usually a **push**, but some rules give ties to the **banker**.
- **Payouts:** Usually even money (1:1). Some games pay bonuses for triples or 4-5-6 (e.g. 2:1 or 3:1).
